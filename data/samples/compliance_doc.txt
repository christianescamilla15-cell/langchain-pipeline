DATA PRIVACY AND COMPLIANCE ASSESSMENT
Organization: DataFlow Analytics Corp.
Assessment Date: December 2024
Framework: GDPR, CCPA, SOC 2 Type II

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This compliance assessment evaluates DataFlow Analytics Corp.'s data handling practices
against GDPR, CCPA, and SOC 2 Type II requirements. The assessment identifies areas of
strong compliance, gaps requiring remediation, and recommendations for improvement.
Overall compliance score: 78/100 (Satisfactory with improvements needed).

2. DATA COLLECTION AND PROCESSING
Current State: The organization processes personal data of approximately 2.4 million
EU residents and 1.8 million California residents. Data processing activities include
customer analytics, behavioral profiling, and marketing automation.

Findings:
- COMPLIANT: Data processing activities are documented in a Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)
- COMPLIANT: Legal basis for processing is established for core business functions
- GAP: Consent management for marketing activities needs improvement
- GAP: Data Processing Impact Assessments (DPIAs) are overdue for 3 processing activities

3. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
Current State: The organization has established procedures for handling data subject requests.

Findings:
- COMPLIANT: Right to access requests are fulfilled within 25 days (deadline: 30 days)
- COMPLIANT: Right to erasure process is functional
- CONCERN: Right to data portability has not been fully tested
- GAP: Automated decision-making opt-out mechanism is not yet implemented

4. SECURITY MEASURES
Current State: Multi-layered security architecture with encryption, access controls, and monitoring.

Findings:
- COMPLIANT: AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit
- COMPLIANT: Role-based access control (RBAC) implemented
- COMPLIANT: 24/7 security monitoring with SOC team
- CONCERN: Penetration testing frequency should increase from annual to quarterly
- GAP: Third-party vendor security assessments are incomplete for 4 vendors

5. DATA BREACH RESPONSE
Current State: Incident response plan is documented and team is trained.

Findings:
- COMPLIANT: 72-hour notification procedure aligned with GDPR Article 33
- COMPLIANT: Breach simulation exercises conducted quarterly
- CONCERN: Communication templates need updating for CCPA requirements

6. AUDIT AND ACCOUNTABILITY
The organization shall maintain comprehensive audit trails for all data processing activities.
Regular internal audits must be conducted at minimum quarterly. External audit readiness
requires complete documentation of all compliance controls and remediation activities.

7. REMEDIATION TIMELINE
- Immediate (30 days): Update consent management platform, complete overdue DPIAs
- Short-term (90 days): Implement automated decision-making opt-out, complete vendor assessments
- Medium-term (180 days): Enhance penetration testing schedule, update CCPA communication templates

8. LIABILITY AND PENALTIES
Non-compliance with GDPR can result in penalties up to 4% of annual global turnover or
EUR 20 million, whichever is higher. CCPA violations carry penalties of $2,500 per violation
(unintentional) or $7,500 per intentional violation. The organization must prioritize
remediation of identified gaps to mitigate compliance risk and potential liability.
